Kedarnath Temple
Lord Shiva

Kedarnath Temple is a sacred Hindu shrine located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. Situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) in the Garhwal Himalayan range near the Mandakini River, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is part of the Char Dham pilgrimage circuit and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are the holiest Shiva temples in India.

Brief history of Temple

The history of Kedarnath, a Hindu pilgrimage site in Uttarakhand, India, is shrouded in antiquity and legend:

Mythological origins
According to Hindu mythology, the Pandavas from the Mahabharata built the Kedarnath temple as an atonement for their sins after the Kurukshetra War. The Pandavas sought out Lord Shiva to help them, but he escaped by turning into a bull. As the Pandavas chased him, the bull broke apart into a corridor, and at Kedarnath, the bull's hump is said to have appeared. The temple is built at the spot where Lord Shiva is believed to have disappeared into the ground in the form of a bull, and the tabernacle is designed to represent Shiva as a Jyotirlinga, an image of cosmic light.

Construction
Some sources say the temple was built by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century, while others credit the Pandavas. The temple has been renovated and rebuilt many times over the centuries, and the current structure dates back to the 19th century. The temple is known for its stone walls and tall pyramid-shaped shikara.

Location
Kedarnath is one of four pilgrimage sites that make up the Uttarkhand Char Dham yatra, or Chota Char Dham yatra. The holy city of Kedarnath is open to the public from May to October or November, but the temple is closed during the monsoon months due to the risk of landslides. The region also experiences cold winters with regular snowfall.



Best time to get here

The best time to visit Kedarnath, a popular pilgrimage site in Uttarakhand, India, is during the summer months of May to June and September to October:

Weather
During these months, the weather is cool with clear skies and mild temperatures, ranging from 8–18°C. Summer mornings are bright and sunny, while nights are cold.

Activities
The pleasant weather is ideal for trekking and sightseeing. April to June is also when the Char Dham Yatra begins, and Kedarnath is filled with pilgrims from across India. June also brings the Badri Kedar Festival, which features traditional customs and performances from Uttarakhand artists.

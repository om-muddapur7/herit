Kandriya Mahadev temple
Lord Shiva

The Kandriya Mahadev Temple, located in Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh, India, is a prominent Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the most revered Shiva shrines in the region and attracts devotees from far and wide. The temple's origins can be traced back to the 12th century, during the Chalukya dynasty. It is believed that the temple was built by a local chieftain named Kandarya.

Brief history of Temple

The history of the Kandariya Mahadev Temple, one of the largest and most ornate temples in Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh, India, is deeply rooted in the Chandela dynasty and showcases the architectural brilliance of the period:

Mythological origins
Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the Kandariya Mahadev Temple is believed to symbolize Mount Kailash, the heavenly abode of Lord Shiva. The temple, like many others in Khajuraho, represents the divine union of masculine and feminine energies, depicted through its intricate carvings and statues. It is considered one of the most important temples dedicated to Shiva in the region and is revered for its divine connection to cosmic energy.

Construction
Built around 1030 CE during the reign of King Vidyadhara of the Chandela dynasty, the Kandariya Mahadev Temple is renowned for its stunning Nagara-style architecture. The temple is adorned with over 800 sculptures, showcasing themes of spirituality, love, and life. Its towering shikhara rises to about 31 meters, symbolizing the mythical Mount Meru. The temple is a prime example of Khajuraho's unique blend of religious devotion and artistic expression.

Location
The Kandariya Mahadev Temple is situated in Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Khajuraho is famous for its group of temples that represent the pinnacle of temple art and architecture in medieval India. The Kandariya Mahadev Temple stands out as the largest and most intricately carved temple in this group.



Best time to get here

The best time to visit the Kandariya Mahadev Temple in Khajuraho, India, is from October to February:

Weather
The weather during these months is pleasant with temperatures ranging between 9–25°C. The cool and comfortable climate makes it ideal for exploring the temple complex and appreciating the detailed carvings and architecture.

Activities
Visitors can enjoy guided tours of the temple, explore the nearby temples in Khajuraho, and participate in the Khajuraho Dance Festival held in February, which showcases classical Indian dance performances against the backdrop of the temples. The peaceful environment and cultural richness of the region make it a rewarding experience for spiritual and architectural enthusiasts.

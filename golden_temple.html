Brief history of Temple

The history of the Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, located in Amritsar, Punjab, India, is intertwined with the spiritual and cultural heritage of Sikhism:

Mythological origins
The foundation of the Golden Temple was laid by the fifth Sikh Guru, Guru Arjan Dev, in 1581. It was designed to be a place of worship for people from all walks of life and all religions to come together in the spirit of equality and devotion. The central shrine, surrounded by a man-made pool known as the Amrit Sarovar, symbolizes purity and spiritual peace.

Construction
The temple was initially completed in 1604, and Guru Arjan Dev installed the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism, in the sanctum. Over the centuries, the temple has undergone several renovations, with the most significant one in the early 19th century when Maharaja Ranjit Singh covered the upper floors of the temple with gold, giving it its iconic appearance. The temple's architecture combines Islamic and Hindu styles, symbolizing the unity of both traditions.

Location
The Golden Temple is located in the heart of Amritsar, Punjab, and is one of the holiest sites in Sikhism. It draws millions of devotees and tourists annually. The temple is open throughout the year and provides free meals to thousands of visitors daily, regardless of their religion or background, through its community kitchen, or "langar."



Best time to get here

The best time to visit the Golden Temple in Amritsar, Punjab, India, is from November to March:

Weather
During these months, the weather is pleasant and cool, with temperatures ranging from 5–20°C. The winter season provides an ideal environment for sightseeing and peaceful meditation around the temple's sacred pool.

Activities
Visitors can participate in the daily rituals, experience the langar, and witness the mesmerizing early morning and evening ceremonies. Special events, such as Guru Nanak Jayanti and Baisakhi, are celebrated with great fervor, drawing large crowds to the temple for prayers and festivities.

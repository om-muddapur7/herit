Dwarka Temple
Lord Krishna

The Dwarkadhish Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna, is a sacred Hindu pilgrimage site in Dwarka, Gujarat. Believed to be over 2500 years old, it's a magnificent structure with intricate carvings. Known as the "Jagat Mandir" (Temple of the World), it's one of the four major pilgrimage sites (Char Dham) for Hindus.

Brief history of Temple

The history of Dwarkadhish Temple, a revered Hindu pilgrimage site in Gujarat, India, is rooted in mythology and history:

Mythological origins
According to Hindu mythology, the Dwarkadhish temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna, who is worshipped here as the "King of Dwarka." After defeating Kansa, Krishna is said to have settled in the city of Dwarka, making it his kingdom. The temple is believed to have been constructed by Krishna’s grandson, Vajranabha, over the land reclaimed from the sea. Dwarka is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and is regarded as one of the holiest places in India.

Construction
The Dwarkadhish temple is an architectural masterpiece, said to have been originally constructed over 2,500 years ago. It has been rebuilt and renovated over the centuries, with the current structure dating back to the 16th century. The temple features a five-story tower and intricate carvings, standing tall on the banks of the Gomti River. The flag atop the temple is changed five times a day and is a symbol of Krishna’s divine protection.

Location
Dwarkadhish Temple is located in the city of Dwarka, Gujarat, on the western coast of India. It is a major pilgrimage site, especially for devotees of Lord Krishna. The temple is easily accessible throughout the year and sees large crowds during Janmashtami, Krishna’s birth celebration.



Best time to get here

The best time to visit Dwarkadhish Temple in Dwarka, Gujarat, is between November and February:

Weather
During these months, the weather is cool and comfortable, with temperatures ranging between 15–25°C, perfect for exploring the temple and the city’s sacred sites.

Activities
November to February is an ideal time for sightseeing, temple visits, and participating in festivals. Janmashtami, the celebration of Lord Krishna’s birth, is a major event at the temple, attracting thousands of devotees. Visitors can also enjoy the serene beaches and nearby attractions like Bet Dwarka.

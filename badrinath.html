Badrinath Temple
Lord Vishnu

Badrinath Temple, nestled amidst the majestic Himalayas in Uttarakhand, India, is one of the most revered Hindu pilgrimage sites. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, the temple is considered one of the Char Dham, the four holy shrines in Hinduism. Its serene location, surrounded by snow-capped peaks and lush valleys, makes it a spiritual haven for devotees.

Brief history of Temple

The history of Badrinath, a revered Hindu pilgrimage site in Uttarakhand, India, is deeply rooted in mythology and spirituality:

Mythological origins
According to Hindu mythology, the Badrinath Temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, who is worshipped here in the form of Badrinarayan. The temple is believed to have been established by the great sage Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century. Legend has it that Lord Vishnu meditated here under a Badri tree (Indian Jujube), and Goddess Lakshmi took the form of the tree to provide him shade from the harsh weather. The site holds immense significance in Hinduism as one of the Char Dham and the Chota Char Dham pilgrimage circuits.

Construction
The present structure of the Badrinath Temple is said to have been built by the Garhwal kings. Over the centuries, the temple has undergone several renovations due to natural disasters and time. The architecture of the temple features a colorful facade with intricate stone carvings, a conical-shaped roof, and a prominent gold gilt roof.

Location
The Badrinath Temple is located in the town of Badrinath, which lies between the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges, along the banks of the Alaknanda River. The temple is situated at an altitude of 3,133 meters and remains open to pilgrims from May to November. During the winter months, the temple is closed due to heavy snowfall.



Best time to get here

The best time to visit Badrinath, a prominent pilgrimage site in Uttarakhand, India, is from May to June and September to October:

Weather
During these months, the weather is pleasant, with temperatures ranging between 10–18°C. The summer months offer clear skies and cool weather, making it ideal for pilgrimage and sightseeing. However, nights can get quite cold due to the temple's high altitude.

Activities
Visitors can take part in the holy rituals, visit the Tapt Kund, a natural hot spring near the temple, and enjoy the picturesque beauty of the surrounding mountains. The Badrinath Temple opens in May with a grand ceremony and remains crowded during the pilgrimage season, especially during the Char Dham Yatra.

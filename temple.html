The Purpose of Temples in India

Temples in India serve various purposes, including spiritual, religious, and cultural. They provide shelter, protection, and support for faithful souls. They also serve as platforms for community gatherings, religious rituals, and the exchange of ideas and knowledge among believers. Additionally, temples can serve as places for worship, learning, and religious ceremonies.

Many people are relocating from their hometowns to cities in pursuit of personal and professional growth. Meanwhile, their families back in the villages continue to uphold the spiritual rituals. City dwellers occasionally return to their roots to maintain these traditions. However, as the younger generation takes over, these connections to their ancestral roots gradually fade, impacting their family's spiritual well-being.
